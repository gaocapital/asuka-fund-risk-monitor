## SECTION 8 — STANDING RULES & SPECIAL CASES
This section consolidates everything that operates as a hard rule, named pattern, or special-case override. Where a rule was already stated in full in Sections 4/5/7, I cross-reference rather than restate; the focus here is the items those sections did not capture or scattered across multiple files.
### 8.1 The Numbered Memory Rules — master list, in order
These are the rules referenced inline throughout the framework files as "memory rule #N". They originated as conversation-level corrections that hardened into standing policy. Reproduce verbatim. Do not delete or renumber: cross-references in the knowledge base depend on stable IDs.
**#1 · "Pure investment" filing language is boilerplate, not intent.**
Japanese EDINET filings frequently state `保有目的: 純投資` (purpose: pure investment) or the longer `純投資及び重要提案行為等を行うこと` (pure investment and important proposal actions as appropriate). The presence of `純投資` does not mean passive. Effissimo, Dalton, LIM, Murakami network, Strategic Capital, Be Brave all file this language and run active campaigns. Behaviour overrides language. Conversely, the addition of `重要提案行為等` (important proposal actions) is the substantive escalation signal — promotes filer language tier from P → R.
**#2 · Dual-vehicle aggregation is mandatory.**
Always sum affiliated vehicles for true economic interest. Canonical pairs: Effissimo Capital Management Pte + SMT Partners Cayman Limited; Dalton Investments + Nippon Active Value Fund (NAVF); the full Murakami umbrella (ATRA, Office Support, City Index Eleventh/First/Third/Maiko/Hospitality/Holdings, Reno, S-Grant, Fortis, C&I, Rebuild, Minami Aoyama Real Estate, MI2, MI5, plus individual filers Yoshiaki / Aya Nomura / Takateru); Ueshima personal + DOE 5 Percent Co. Ltd + Naturali Co. Ltd. Always read the 共同保有者 section before treating any individual filer string as the full economic interest.
**#3 · The 2/3 supermajority threshold is the binding bar for special resolutions.**
Articles-of-incorporation changes, mergers, spin-offs, share buyback authorisations beyond a certain scale all require ≥2/3 of voting shares present at the AGM. A 33.4% blocking stake is the canonical activist threshold; sub-33.4% can still pressure but cannot veto. A near-miss vote (47%+ support) with minority-shareholder solidarity is still a loud signal and may force a board response within 12 months.
**#4 · April record date for June AGMs.**
Japan's AGM cycle concentrates 80%+ of listed companies in June. Record date is typically late March / early April; shareholder proposals must be filed eight weeks before the AGM date, putting the late-February-to-early-April window as the highest-signal escalation period. May convocation notices (招集通知) carry the AGM agenda and shareholder-proposal text — scan TDNet daily from early May.
**#5 · TSE PBR < 1.0x is a screener, not a thesis.**
The TSE 2023–2026 capital-efficiency programme pressures sub-1.0x-PBR companies, but the thesis requires (a) an identified catalyst, (b) an activist or engaged shareholder with a track record, (c) a fundamental case for why the discount closes. Not every cheap Japanese stock is investable — Konica Minolta (4902) was passed despite Effissimo 9.55% + PBR 0.6x because the operational gap was structural, not capital-allocation, and the company lacked the balance-sheet capacity to deliver the activist's typical demands.
**#6 · PWER is the north star. 20% minimum entry.**
No position below 20% absolute PWER unless serving a specific defensive role (ballast, hedge, uncorrelated stream — and the fund's long-only mandate means hedges and shorts are not used). New additions must be accretive to portfolio-level weighted-average PWER and must displace the lowest-PWER incumbent — capital comes from explicit trims, not from cash accumulation. v9 added the *annualized PWER ≥ 15%* parallel gate to catch multi-year campaigns whose absolute PWER passes only on horizon length (Tamron-style time-decay risk).
**#7 · Sizing discipline is non-negotiable.**
Max single position 12% NAV (L1 only, highest conviction). Total positions 14–18. 100% deployed target (5–10% cash buffer per v11 acceptable). 25% per-activist concentration cap. 50% per-event-cluster cap. Mega-cap activist cluster ≤6% NAV. L4 ARB sleeve sized 1–3% per position. Use trims to fund new entries; never accumulate cash.
**#8 · Activist WAC Cross-Check (v8 origin, v10.3 amended).**
For any activist co-investment thesis, PWER at current price must be cross-checked against the principal activist's weighted-average cost basis. If current price is **>15% above activist WAC**, co-investment edge is gone; PWER must be justified on standalone event-driven terms only. v9.1 tightened the threshold to **+10%** for dual-vehicle structures. v10.3 amended the input from disclosed WAC to **True-WAC** (computed per the §3 ten-step concealment scan). Sanyo Denki Apr 24 2026 application: current price ¥5,660 = +50–65% above SC WAC ¥3,400–3,800 → queued add withdrawn.
**#9 · WAC Inversion Rule.**
When the principal activist is a *net seller* (aggregate stake declining from peak), the co-investment framework inverts entirely — the activist is *distributing to you*, not co-investing with you. PWER must reflect distribution dynamics regardless of WAC arithmetic. Murakami Stage 5 (buyback-then-exit) confirmed when ALL five fire: aggregate stake declining from peak + completed buyback + stock near 52-week high + price above consensus + peak stalled below 33% veto threshold (see Murakami Stage 5 pattern below).
**#10 · Murakami Internal Redistribution Rule.**
High change-report numbers (No. 15+) signal a mature campaign in late stages. New individual-vehicle 5%+ filings in late campaigns often = internal transfer between Murakami vehicles, **not** fresh accumulation. Always read the joint-holder section and sum aggregate group direction before treating any single-vehicle increase as bullish. Exedy Change Report No. 24 (Feb 2026) is canonical: City Index First filed +2.85pp = receiving stock from Reno (−3.65pp), aggregate group −1.06pp.
**#11 · ATRA Direct-Filer Tripwire.**
ATRA usually sits silently above the operating vehicles in the Murakami umbrella and does not appear in the 共同保有者 section. When ATRA appears as direct co-filer (alongside CIE / Aya Nomura / Office Support / Reno), it signals high-conviction Murakami deployment. Treat as +1 sizing tier upgrade. Wavelock HD November 2025 (combined 14.93%) is the canonical precedent. Rengo 2026 5% combined currently displays the bullish ATRA-present pattern.
**#12 · Optimal Entry Window T1–T2.**
For shadow-follow setups, T0 is the first 5%+ filing date; T1 is days 1–14; T2 is days 14–60. T1–T2 captures maximum risk-adjusted return. The pop at T0 represents disclosure alpha (mechanical re-rating), not campaign-resolution alpha. Entering past T2 + 8–15% premium-to-activist-WAC typically fails Rule #8 / #9.
**#13 · Profit-Taking on Recalibrated PWER, Not Nominal Gain.**
Forward PWER compression is the correct trim trigger, not the absolute return on entry. A position up +40% that still carries +30% forward PWER is held; a position up +15% whose forward PWER has compressed to +5% is trimmed. Activist continued accumulation is the primary signal *against* trimming; selling against active accumulation by the principal is incorrect.
**#14 · Oasis Structural Failure Pattern.**
The three-factor danger signal: founding family bloc ≥30% + large-cap diffuse ownership + consumer/pharma sector concentration. When all three fire, Oasis campaigns historically fail. Hokuetsu, Kao, Ain Holdings, Kusuri-No-Aoki, DIC are the documented cases. Tokyo Steel (Ikegaya family ~30%) currently sits in this danger zone. Yakult Honsha (2267) is the active 2026 case to watch — Morningstar FV ¥1,311 vs ¥2,600 price = the activism premium that may not close. **Reject regardless of headline PWER.** This is the canonical drag-mode for the strategy.
**#15 · SOTP-as-Floor Caution.**
SOTP is a point estimate, not a hard asset floor. Realisation requires activist force or management cooperation; transformation costs consume book value; conglomerate discounts can persist for years. Do not over-correct PWER upward solely on the logic that SOTP equals market price. The Ricoh digital-services unit framing is the canonical case where SOTP-as-floor is valuable as conviction support but cannot drive sizing on its own.
**#16 · Liquidity Discipline.**
Position sizing always cross-checked against average daily volume (¥-ADV). Hard caps enforced regardless of thesis quality. Inui Global Logistics: 1.5% max due to ¥159M ADV ceiling. Miyoshi Oil & Fat (4404): micro-cap liquidity-constrained, hold at 1.5%, no add. L4 ARB sleeve has its own ADV gate — bear floor reservation only meaningful if exit liquidity supports.
**#17 · Capital Redeployment is Explicit.**
Exits and trims are always linked to funded entries. Daito Trust trim → fund Kansai Paint. Foster Electric trim → fund Teijin. Alfresa 2% → 0 and Daito Trust 2% → 1% → fund Yellow Hat 3%. Capital sits in cash only as a transition buffer (acceptable 5–10% per v11).
**#18 · Catalyst-Calendar Anchoring.**
Primary rebalancing events are catalyst-driven, not calendar-driven: June AGM season, April record dates, quarterly earnings, EDINET filing triggers. Monthly / weekly rebalance discipline is a *check*, not a *prompt*. The PWER stack is a discrete signal that moves on filings/AGMs/escalations, not a continuous rebalancing input.
**#19 · Portfolio Correlation Management.**
Highly correlated positions are sized as a single basket. The Ueshima / DOE 5% basket (Gun Ei, Tayca, Fujikura Kasei, Miyoshi) is treated as one position for sizing purposes given high correlation to a single activist-campaign outcome. Per-activist cap (25%) applies to basket sum, not individual lines.
**#20 · Stock-Specific Hard-Stop Framework (May 13 2026).**
Every position refresh must run a hard-stop check at the top of the response, citing the name's own stops. Five stop types: (a) anchor activist reduction below threshold; (b) fundamental break (profit warning, guidance cut, accounting issue); (c) price gap above ceiling without catalyst; (d) WAC inversion >15% above anchor; (e) name-specific entry tripwire. Any hit triggers a ⛔ HARD STOP ALERT header and prescribed action (trim/exit/freeze). Per-name stops live in `position-book-current.md` per-position metadata. Skipping this step is a memo-validity failure.
**#21 · Date Primary-Source Rule.**
Earnings dates, AGM dates, ex-dividend dates, TOB resolution dates, and other binary-event dates must be sourced from the company IR calendar primary (e.g. `pa-consul.co.jp/en/ir/calendar`). Aggregator dates (Investing.com, Yahoo Finance, Bloomberg event tags) are secondary; if conflict, IR calendar wins. Codify: `catalyst_date_source = "ir_calendar"` is required to lock binary-window classification. Plus Alpha Consulting 4071 May 3 2026 was the precipitating case (aggregator showed May 15, IR calendar showed May 14 — IR won).
**#22 · EDINET XBRL First Rule.**
For any 大量保有報告書 / 変更報告書 data, attempt EDINET primary fetch (`disclosure.edinet-fsa.go.jp`, doc ID `S100XXXX`) **before** aggregators. Aggregators — `kabupro.jp`, `irbank.net`, `maonline.jp`, `ufocatch.com`, `note.com` summaries, Yahoo Finance Japan retail forums — are fallback only; aggregator data must be stamped with the corresponding EDINET doc ID or flagged `STALE_INPUTS`. Joint-holder section, exact share counts, WAC math, and `重要提案行為等` language all require XBRL — aggregators truncate. Stamp `edinet_doc_id = S100XXXX` is required to lock an action signal. Memory rule #22 is binding per `edinet-monitoring-protocol.md` §9 single-source-of-truth hierarchy.
**#23 · PB Block-Warehousing Pattern.**
Major prime brokers (Goldman International, Nomura International PLC) warehouse blocks for activists ahead of disclosure. Filing signature: (i) PB files NEW 5%+ on a Japan target; (ii) within 30–90 days, an in-universe activist files NEW or ±pt on the same target; (iii) PB unwinds concurrent with activist's increase. Confirmed April 2026 cases: Teijin (GS NEW Mar 31 → −3.26pt Apr 22 vs Effissimo +1.73pt same week); Sankei REIT (GS + Nomura −7pt vs CIE +5.96pt over 8 days); Casio (Nomura group −9pt+ vs 3D NEW 5.03%). Operational rule: any PB NEW 5%+ on a Japan name is a 30–90-day forward-looking activist filing screen. The pipeline maintains a separate `pb_warehouse_log` table with auto-expire at 90 days unless an in-universe filer registers.
**#24 · Price Freshness Rule.**
Quoted spot prices and FX rates must be stamped with source-date, and any stamp >2 trading days old is treated as `STALE` and re-fetched before sizing decisions. Morningstar / Investing.com / TradingView frequently serve cached snapshots with weeks-old data presented as current; three sources matching value + same old stamp = one cached snapshot, not three confirmations. JP equities: prefer `kabutan.jp` intraday or Yahoo Finance Japan with today's stamp. Teijin May 3 2026 was the precipitating case where stale-price contamination nearly drove a sizing error.
**#25 · PBR Data Freshness Rule.**
Any PBR figure in `filer-roster-japan.md` or position notes must carry a date stamp matching (a) WAC date or (b) explicit refresh date. Untagged PBR = `STALE_INPUTS`, treated as unverified, cannot drive a REJECT. The Musashi 7220 GMO miss (May 2026) traced to a post-rally PBR 2.45x reading contaminating the WAC-date PBR which was ~0.85x sub-book. Always re-stamp PBR on each filer-roster refresh; tag `pbr_as_of_date`.
**#26 · Data Quarantine Rule.**
When stored fields conflict with verified EDINET or external price data, apply `DATA_QUARANTINE` override (red ⛔) and reset corrupted fields. The auto-tilt engine has produced phantom data in the past — 3034 QOL stored stake 17.73% (real 8.69% per Will Field Oct 2025); calibrated price ¥1,369 (real spot ~¥1,790). Corruption signature: `stake_confirmation` tilt + missing `last_filing.date`. Same flag applied historically to 7725 / 3776 / 4493. Always cross-check stake % vs EDINET before treating as actionable.
**#27 · Fact-Check Discipline Rule (post-Money Forward May 2026).**
Separate `CONFIRMED FACTS` (EDINET / IR / TDNet with date) from `INFERENCE` ("consistent with activist behaviour"). Five common traps:
(a) AGM timing — verify vs fiscal year-end + 定款; never default to June. Money Forward FY-end Nov 30 → AGM Jan–Feb, not June.
(b) Founder ownership % — pull from 有価証券報告書 大株主 section; do not estimate.
(c) Quarterly financials — official 決算短信 PDF, not aggregator.
(d) Legal thresholds (15% board, 33.4% block) — phrase as "historically gets," not "entitled to."
(e) Price anchor — one verified date+source, not a band.
**#28 · X-Bull Probability Discipline (post-Plus Alpha May 3 2026).**
X-Bull (strategic / MBO scenarios) probability reflects structural M&A precedent, not optimism. Founder-led growth SaaS: cap 10% (no MBO precedent). Auto-parts / conglomerate with corporate cross-shareholder: 15–20% (Mazda / keiretsu unwind precedents). RE / J-REIT under TOB: 15–25% (CIE Sankei precedent). Sub-book industrials with cash: 10–15%. Bear weight inversely scales with founder-coalition tightness: >30% bloc = bear +5pp.
**#29 · Daily Freshness Sweep Standing Order (May 15 2026).**
Every Tokyo trading day at ≥17:00 JST run a 4-pass over all current book positions: (1) px ≤ 2 days old; (2) WAC delta T1–T8; (3) EDINET 7-day window + TDNet 3-day window; (4) verdict reconcile. Output `sweep-log/YYYY-MM-DD-sweep.md` every run. HTML dashboard increments only on verdict change. Protocol document: `sweep/daily-freshness-sweep-protocol.md`. Skipped days are logged, not silent. Absorbs the earlier `REFRESH RULE` — a signal needs px ≤ 3 days + EDINET ≤ 7 days + news ≤ 7 days, else `STALE_INPUTS`.
**#30 · Activist-as-Stake-Confirmation Rule (Musashi 7220 May 2026).**
When the L4 strategic-source re-tag fires a non-canonical source (e.g. SUB+TOB on a name where the archetype-fit verdict was REJECT), the activist downgrades from thesis driver to institutional validation of the stake. PWER ceiling adjusts: canonical archetype-fit = filer's Bloomberg ACTV mean × 1.5; re-tagged with L2+L3 support = sector/structural mean (typically 25–35%); re-tagged *without* L2+L3 support = REJECT stands. Musashi 7220 GMO filing = SUB+TOB re-tag with macro+fundamentals support → BUY at L3 starter at WAC.
### 8.2 The Eight v10.3 Standing Rules (concealment-aware WAC framework)
Reproduce verbatim from `asuka-framework-extension-v10_3.md §8`. These operate **alongside** memory rules #1–#30, not within them.
1. **The concealment-aware WAC rule.** All WAC cross-checks (memory rule #8 +15%, v9.1 +10% dual-vehicle, v10.1 Gate 4) apply against True-WAC computed per the ten-step concealment scan, not disclosed WAC.
2. **The mandatory concealment scan.** Every shadow-follow candidate at T0 and every refresh of an existing position triggers the §2 ten-step protocol. Skipping the scan is a memo-validity failure.
3. **The conservative-reference rule.** When multiple True-WAC candidates apply, default to the highest of the candidate references.
4. **The override-down memory rule.** Strategic Capital filings on names with prior UGS Sunshine-LP joint history get auto-tagged concealment band 1–2 regardless of clean current-filing reading (meito 2207 standing condition).
5. **The pre-5% silent accumulation rule.** When transaction-date span > 60 days on a first 大量保有報告書, the entry-price reference is the cross-day price, not the disclosed period-weighted WAC.
6. **The vehicle-stasis rule.** Three consecutive change reports with WAC stable within ±¥10 (or ±0.5%) despite >15% intra-period price range = paper-only redistribution; ignore that vehicle's disclosed WAC.
7. **The internal-transfer-doesn't-improve-edge rule.** When C1 fires, the disclosed group-blended WAC may move favourably but the shadow follower's marginal entry is still at market. Use lead-filer WAC.
8. **The PB-warehouse anchor rule.** When C7 fires, the activist's intent-formation price is anchored to the PB warehousing-window VWAP, not the disclosed handoff WAC — favourable for thesis durability but not for follower entry economics.

### 8.4 Hard Stops — universal (per memory rule #20)
Every position must carry name-specific hard stops in the position-book metadata. Five universal stop types:
(a) **Anchor activist reduction below threshold** — e.g. Effissimo trims Ricoh below 22%, Strategic Capital reduces below 13% at any name → exit signal.
(b) **Fundamental break** — profit warning, downward guidance revision, accounting irregularity, Special Alert listing → trim or exit pending re-underwrite. Inui Global Logistics FY profit warning (April 2026) = reduce to 0.5% or exit.
(c) **Price gap above ceiling without catalyst** — e.g. position +25% above thesis target without a triggering event → trim into strength.
(d) **WAC inversion >15%** — current price >15% above activist WAC and no Gate 1.5 / Realized-Mode-1 carry exemption → freeze adds, evaluate trim. Ricoh May 2026: spot ¥1,610 vs Effissimo WAC ¥1,200 = +34% → downgrade ADD → HOLD.
(e) **Name-specific entry tripwire** — pre-defined in position memo. E.g. Square Enix bear-case ¥2,240 (post-AGM-defeat scenario), Sanyo Denki drops below ¥4,500, Yakult Honsha do-not-initiate without clearance.
### 8.5 Edge Cases — how each is handled
**Data quarantine (memory rule #26).** Phantom stake / phantom price / missing `last_filing.date` triggers immediate red ⛔ override. Affected fields reset to `null`; do not act on `DATA_QUARANTINE` rows until human PM verifies against EDINET XBRL. Standing affected names from earlier sweeps: 3034 QOL Holdings, 7725 Inter Action, 3776 Broadband Tower, 4493 Cyber Security Cloud.
**Custody veiling pending verification.** Foreign nominee strings (Northern Trust, JP Morgan numerical aliases, Citigroup, State Street, BNY Mellon, BBH) without XBRL filer-entity resolution land in the `BENEFICIAL_OWNER_PENDING_VERIFICATION` queue. Do NOT compute WAC or set PWER until resolution. Aged 30+ days = escalate to human PM for manual XBRL lookup.
**Foreign-domiciled vehicle late incorporation.** When a new joint-holder corporate filer appears in 共同保有者 with an incorporation date within 30 calendar days of first appearance, flag **C2** (new family-member vehicle late campaign) per v10.3 §2 Step 8. Query `国税庁法人番号公表サイト` for incorporation date; cache in `vehicle_registry.json`. Does not apply to Murakami family individuals (Aya Nomura, Takateru, Yoshiaki direct filings).
**Single-PM concentration risk.** GMO-Usonian sleeve sits under Drew Edwards' judgement; Optex (8% NAV) + Oisix (5%) + Musashi 7220 watch concentrates 15–18% NAV under one PM. Treat as single-decision-maker exposure and cap sleeve at ≤15% combined NAV unless explicit PM override.
**DOE 5% basket heterogeneous FYE (Miyoshi correction May 14 2026).** Do NOT size the Ueshima / DOE 5% basket as a single June-AGM calendar. Miyoshi Oil & Fat (4404) FYE is December 31 — 2026 AGM held late March. Gun Ei, Tayca, Fujikura Kasei each carry separate FYE / AGM dates. Per-name FYE verification required before basket-level catalyst-window classification.
**Mega-cap activist cluster cap.** Total exposure to mega-cap activism (Palliser at Ajinomoto, TOTO, Tokyu Fudosan, Keisei, Japan Post; ValueAct at Olympus, Money Forward) capped at 6% NAV combined. Mega-cap activism underperforms (Palliser n=68 mean +21.2%, only 27% >+30%); concentration limit prevents the strategy from sliding into a Tier-2 mega-cap factor exposure.
**Hidden-Tech Trigger arrived-too-late.** PAH-A 5-gate fires on TOTO retroactively but the +60% YoY rally to ¥5,425 by Apr 30 2026 leaves no shadow-follow edge. Standing rule: when PAH-A fires post-rally, log to "arrived too late" precedent file; re-entry only on meaningful retrace (typically 25%+ pullback) or fresh activist escalation. TOTO is the canonical example.
