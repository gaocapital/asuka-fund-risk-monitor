## 3. PORTFOLIO ARCHITECTURE

The Asuka Fund maintains a **concentrated, fully-deployed portfolio** structured in conviction layers:

**L1 — High-Conviction Binary Catalysts (Target: ~50–60% of portfolio)**

- Action bias: ADD on dips, HOLD at full size, TRIM only if catalyst passed without resolution

- Activist stake recently disclosed (< 6 months), catalyst 3–9 months out, thesis developing

PWER = Σ (Scenario Probability × Scenario Return)

### Portfolio Sizing Rules

- New position entry: 3% (L2) or 5–8% (L3/GMO sleeve)
- Total positions: 14–18 (diversified but concentrated)

## 5. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK — HOW TO RESEARCH A NEW SIGNAL

When a new investor stake is disclosed or an existing stake is increased, follow this sequence:

### Step 1: Identify the Activist's Playbook
- Match the investor to their known engagement mode (see Section 2)
- Check historical Japan campaigns: typical entry size, escalation timeline, proposal types, success rate
- Note: "Pure investment / pure holding purpose" in Japan filings does NOT mean passive — it is standard boilerplate, especially for Effissimo, Dalton, and LIM

### Step 2: Fundamental Setup — Is This a Genuine Value Trap or Value Opportunity?
- PBR vs. sector peers (TSE is pressuring PBR < 1.0x companies explicitly)
- Conglomerate discount analysis: identify core business vs. non-core drag
- Cross-shareholding structure: identify TSE-reform-sensitive cross-holders who face pressure to sell
- ROIC / ROE vs. cost of capital; capital allocation history
- Net cash / balance sheet optionality

### Step 3: Shareholder Structure Deep Dive
- EDINET large shareholder report (5% threshold disclosures)
- Cross-shareholder map: which corporates hold stakes that TSE reform targets?
- Dual-vehicle structures (e.g., Dalton + NAVF) — combined economic interest often significantly larger than individual filings suggest
- Family / founding shareholder dynamics (blocking minority, MBO risk, loyalty to management)

### Step 4: Catalyst Mapping
Identify and date concrete catalysts:
- **AGM season (June):** Shareholder proposals require April record date filing; escalation window is Feb–April
- **EDINET formal 5%+ filing:** For "meaningful stake" investors, formal EDINET entry = re-rating signal
- **Stake increases crossing round numbers:** 5% → 10% → 15% → 20% → 1/3 (veto territory) are all escalation signals
- **Asset disposals / spin-offs / buybacks** announced by the company in response to activist pressure
- **Board composition changes:** Activist-nominated directors accepted or rejected
- **Ex-dividend dates and earnings:** For L3 compounders, use earnings catalysts to time entry

### Step 5: PWER Construction
Build a scenario table:

| Scenario | Probability | Price Target | Return from Current |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base (activist partial win) | X% | ¥Y | +Z% |
| Bull (full restructuring) | X% | ¥Y | +Z% |
| Extreme Bull (MBO / strategic sale) | X% | ¥Y | +Z% |
| Bear (stalemate, earnings miss) | X% | ¥Y | -Z% |

**PWER = Σ(P × R)** — must exceed 20% to justify entry; must exceed current lowest-PWER L2/L3 position to justify displacement.

### Step 6: Portfolio Integration Decision

## 6. JAPAN IDIOSYNCRATIC ALPHA FRAMEWORK

These are the structural alpha sources specific to Japan that make this strategy work. Always evaluate positions against these lenses:

### 6.1 TSE Governance Reform Tailwind
The Tokyo Stock Exchange's 2023–2026 push to improve capital efficiency (targeting PBR > 1x, ROE > cost of equity) creates a **systemic re-rating environment**. Companies with PBR < 1.0x face explicit TSE pressure and shareholder scrutiny. This is the single biggest Japan-specific macro tailwind for the strategy.

### 6.2 Cross-Shareholding Unwind
Japan corporates historically held equity stakes in each other as loyalty/relationship capital. TSE reform + corporate governance codes are accelerating the unwind of these positions. When a cross-holder is pressed to sell (e.g., Sony selling Tamron, or a regional bank selling industrial stakes):
- It creates float expansion and re-rating
- The target company can use proceeds for buybacks
- Activist pressure amplifies the timeline

### 6.3 Conglomerate Discount / Sum-of-the-Parts Unlock
Japanese companies routinely operate in 4–8 unrelated business segments (medical + chemicals + logistics + cosmetics, etc.). The market applies a conglomerate discount. Activists push for:
- Non-core segment divestitures
- Spin-offs or IPOs of subsidiaries
- Capital reallocation to core business
The SOTP upside on a clean break-up is often 50–100%+ vs. current conglomerate discount.

### 6.4 AGM Season Concentration
Japan's AGM calendar is heavily concentrated in June (80%+ of listed companies). This creates:
- **April record date** as the activist proposal filing deadline — watch February–April for escalation signals
- **Binary catalyst windows** for L1 positions: resolution pass/fail at June AGMs
- **Post-AGM dislocation:** Stocks that lose AGM votes but retain activist overhang often re-base and offer re-entry

### 6.5 MBO / Take-Private Optionality
Founding family + corporate cross-shareholders controlling > 50% creates a structural MBO risk/opportunity:
- **Asymmetric upside:** Activist blocking positions (15–33%) force higher MBO premiums
- **Effissimo "Mode 3" pattern:** Accumulate to veto level ahead of anticipated low-ball bid; demand premium
- Monitor: companies with controlling family + sub-market-value subsidiaries + no shareholder return commitment

### 6.6 Dual-Vehicle Structure Awareness
Activists running multiple vehicles (Dalton + NAVF, Effissimo + SMT Partners) can hold combined positions significantly above individual disclosure thresholds. Always sum all associated entities for true economic interest:
- Combined position often reveals near-veto or special resolution blocking capability
- Aggregate holdings > 33.4% = de facto veto on special resolutions
- Aggregate > 50% = effective control

## 9. CRITICAL RULES

1. **"Pure investment" in a Japanese filing does not mean passive.** It is standard boilerplate. Effissimo, Dalton, LIM, and others have all run active campaigns after filing this exact language.

2. **Combined entity stakes always supersede individual filings.** Dalton + NAVF, Effissimo + SMT Partners — always sum.

3. **The 2/3 supermajority threshold is the critical bar for special resolutions** (articles of incorporation, mergers, spin-offs). A near-miss (47%+ support) with minority shareholder solidarity is still a loud signal.

4. **AGM record dates in Japan are typically early April for June AGMs.** Activist proposals must be filed before the record date. The February–April window is the highest-signal period for escalation monitoring.

5. **TSE PBR < 1.0x is a screener, not a thesis.** The thesis requires an identified catalyst, an activist or engaged shareholder with a track record, and a fundamental case for why the discount closes. Not every cheap Japanese stock is investable.

6. **PWER is the north star for portfolio decisions.** No position below 20% PWER unless it serves a specific role (defensive ballast, hedge, uncorrelated return stream). New additions must be accretive to portfolio-level PWER.

7. **Sizing discipline is non-negotiable.** Max 12% single position, 100% deployed, 14–18 positions. Use trims to fund new entries; do not accumulate cash.
